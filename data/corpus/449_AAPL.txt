Apple (NASDAQ:AAPL) manufacturing services supplier Jabil Circuits (NYSE:JBL) reported better-than-expected fiscal fourth-quarter 2015 results early Friday and gave guidance above Wall Street consensus, boosting its stock.

Jabil reported revenue of $4.68 billion, up 15% from the year-earlier quarter, beating Wall Street estimates of $4.55 billion. It posted earnings per share minus items of 53 cents, up from 5 cents and topping the consensus of 45 cents.

Jabil stock rose as high as 22.99 Friday, up 17% and at a three-month high, before ending the day at 21.88, up 12.2%. The stock is working on the right side of a five-month consolidation.

Jabil provides electronic manufacturing services, designing and making products for Apple, Cisco Systems (NASDAQ:CSCO), IBM (NYSE:IBM), Hewlett-Packard (NYSE:HPQ) and others. A big chunk of fiscal Q4 growth came from Jabil's diversified electronics manufacturing services business, the segment that includes Apple. Jabil makes casings for the iPhone. In fiscal 2015, Apple accounted for 24% of Jabil's revenue.

Apple on Friday began in-store sales of its new iPhone 6S and 6S Plus.

RBC Capital Markets analyst Amit Daryanani, in a research note, called the Q4 results "impressive." Daryanani rates Jabil Circuit stock sector perform, with a price target of 25.

For the current fiscal Q1, Jabil forecasts revenue of $5.1 billion to $5.3 billion, above the consensus of $4.9 billion. It projects EPS of 72 cents to 88 cents, vs. the consensus of 61 cents.
