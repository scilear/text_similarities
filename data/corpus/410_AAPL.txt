Led by consumer electronics giant Apple (NASDAQ:AAPL), technology companies continue to have the most valuable brands in the world, though athletic gear maker Nike (NYSE:NKE) and industrial giant General Electric (NYSE:GE) also earned top spots.

Apple's brand is valued at $170.28 billion, up 43% from a year ago, Interbrand said. Technology companies accounted for six of the top 10 and a third of the $1.71 trillion value of all brands on the market tracker's latest top 100 list, released Sunday.

Apple was No. 1 for the third year in a row, followed by Alphabet's (NASDAQ:GOOGL) Google, valued at $120.31 billion (up 12%), and Coca-Cola (NYSE:KO) with $78.42 billion (down 4%).

Microsoft (NASDAQ:MSFT) was No. 4 with $67.67 billion (up 11%), and IBM (NYSE:IBM) was No. 5 with $65.1 billon (down 10%).

Apple shares closed up 0.4% in Monday trading on the stock market today. Alphabet rose 2.2%. Coca-Cola gained 1.5%, Microsoft 2.3% and IBM 3%.

Rounding out the top 10 were Toyota (NYSE:TM) valued at $49.05 billion (up 16%), Samsung at $45.3 billion (unchanged), General Electric at $42.28 billion (down 7%), McDonald's (NYSE:MCD) at $39.81 billion (down 6%), and Amazon.com (NASDAQ:AMZN) at $37.95 billion (up 29%).

Among other notable names, entertainment and media conglomerate Disney (NYSE:DIS) was No. 13 with $36.5 billion, and Nike was No. 17 with $23.1 billion.

Five brands that saw the steepest increases in value were Facebook (NASDAQ:FB) (up 54%), Apple, Amazon, Paris-based luxury goods company Hermes (up 22%), and Nissan Motor (OTCPK:NSANY) which rose 22% to No. 41.
