NEW YORK (TheStreet) -- Apple (AAPL - Get Report) shares are rallying 1.03% to $110.18 in pre-market trading as the tech giant began offering Apple Music, its music streaming service, in China today, Bloomberg reports.

"Customers in China love the App Store and have made it our largest market in the world for app downloads," said Eddy Cue, Apple's senior VP of Internet software and services.

Following a three-month trial membership, Apple will start charging a monthly subscription fee of 10 yuan or $1.57, Bloomberg noted.

In addition to the music streaming service, movies and electronic books will also be available to consumers in China.

Separately, according to research firm Wristly, the average selling price of the new Apple Watch is $529. Depending on what models users buy, whether it's a sport model or the one with solid gold designs, prices can range from $350 to $17,000, CNBC.com said.
