General Electric Co. GE, +2.53% said Thursday it has agreed to sell the transportation finance unit of GE Capital to BMO Financial Group BMO, +1.42% as the conglomerate continues to shed financial businesses in a quest to increase profits and focus on the industrial side. With this transaction, the total for 2015 sales is approximately $85 billion, GE said. "We expect to reach sales agreements for the majority of our U.S. businesses by the end of this year and the sale of the Transportation Finance business, one of our core commercial lending and leasing businesses, gets us closer to that goal," GE Capital Chief Executive Keith Sherin said in a statement. Terms of the deal were not disclosed. Shares of GE rose 0.1% in late trading after ending the regular session up 0.5%.
